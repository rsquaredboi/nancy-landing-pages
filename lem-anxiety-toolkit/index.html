The Full Ranking

8
Acupressure Mat
Pranamat / various
★★☆☆☆ 2.5/5

Price: $80–130  |  Time to effect: 15–20 min

Painful learning curve. Most testers quit after a week. The theory is sound — pressure point stimulation to release tension — but the compliance rate was terrible. Two of our twelve testers genuinely liked it. The other ten described it as “torture cosplaying as wellness.”

Drug-free Reusable Uncomfortable 15+ min required Not portable
7
CBD Gummies
Various brands
★★★☆☆ 2.8/5

Price: $30–60/month  |  Time to effect: 30–60 min

Inconsistent. Some testers felt calmer, others felt nothing. The unregulated nature of the CBD industry means wildly varying quality between brands, and even between batches from the same brand. Several testers reported next-morning grogginess. The ongoing monthly cost adds up quickly for uncertain results.

Easy to take Tastes good Unregulated Inconsistent effects Drowsy next morning Ongoing cost
6
Fidget Toys
Pop-its, stress balls, fidget cubes
★★★☆☆ 3.0/5

Price: $5–25  |  Time to effect: Immediate (momentary)

Momentary distraction, not stress relief. Good for meetings, useless for insomnia. The satisfaction of popping a bubble or clicking a cube is real — but it evaporates the moment you stop. It doesn’t address the root cause of stress or create any lasting physiological change. Think of it as a Band-Aid for your hands.

Cheap Portable Immediate Doesn't address root cause Momentary only No lasting effect
5
Journaling
Morning Pages / Bullet Journal
★★★☆☆ 3.3/5

Price: $15 (journal)  |  Time to effect: 2–4 weeks

Genuinely helpful for processing thoughts. The research behind expressive writing is solid, and several testers reported feeling clearer after consistent morning pages. But journaling doesn’t address somatic stress — your body still holds tension even when your mind has processed it. It’s a mental tool for a physical problem.

Free / cheap Therapeutic Evidence-based Time-consuming Requires consistency Doesn't address physical tension
4
Meditation App
Calm / Headspace
★★★☆☆ 3.5/5

Price: $70–100/year ($6–8/month)  |  Time to effect: 10–20 min per session, benefits build over weeks

Works if you stick with it. Most testers didn’t. The irony of needing discipline to reduce stress is not lost on us. The evidence base for meditation is strong, and the three testers who maintained a daily practice for the full six weeks showed genuine improvement. But a 75% dropout rate tells its own story.

Evidence-based Convenient Large library Requires time & discipline Subscription Slow onset
3
Stress Supplements
Magnesium, Ashwagandha, L-Theanine
★★★☆☆ 3.5/5

Price: $25–45/month  |  Time to effect: 1–3 weeks

Magnesium was the standout. Specifically magnesium glycinate before bed — several testers noticed better sleep within 10 days. Ashwagandha showed mixed results. L-Theanine was subtle but pleasant. But supplements are slow and gradual — you won’t feel “unstressed” after taking one. And the ongoing monthly cost for quality supplements adds up.

Evidence for magnesium Easy to incorporate Slow onset Inconsistent Ongoing cost Variable quality
2
Weighted Blanket
Bearaby / YnM
★★★★☆ 4.0/5

Price: $80–250  |  Time to effect: 15–30 min

Our second favorite. Genuinely calming. Deep pressure stimulation is well-researched — it activates the parasympathetic nervous system, essentially telling your body it’s safe to relax. Most testers reported falling asleep faster. But it’s passive — you just lie there and hope it works. Hot in summer. Can’t travel with it. And for acute stress during the day, it’s not practical.
